Set up your media library

Your images stay in your own Google Drive — you keep ownership. Brand Autopilot works from three folders, and can create them for you right now:

📁 Brand Autopilot / Paper Meridian ├─ 📁 Products one subfolder per product (P0001, P0002 …) │ └─ 📁 P0001 │ ├─ 📁 Pin room-scene images for Pinterest — the product styled in a real space │ ├─ 📁 Web .webp product images for your website's product page │ ├─ 📁 Stu .webp studio shots — white or plain color background │ └─ 📁 Review real customer photos (.webp) — social proof for blog & email ├─ 📁 Inspiration room scenes & moodboards — no product required └─ 📁 Video intake drop short clips here for TikTok / Reels / Shorts
🗂Each routine reads only the folder it needs: pins come from Pin, your product-page gallery from Web, catalog & ads from Stu, trust content from Review. Mixing them up is the #1 beginner mistake — a studio shot posted as a pin underperforms badly.
📸What makes a Pin image work: show the product inside a styled room, taking up 15–35% of the frame. On the brand this system was built on, room-scene images out-performed plain close-ups roughly 50×. Never upload the same image twice — the routines enforce this with a dedupe ledger.
One click — we'll drop a “what goes here” note inside each folder.
