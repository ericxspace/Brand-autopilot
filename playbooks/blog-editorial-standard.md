# Blog Editorial Standard — the 90/10 subtle-sell rule

*Read by the 02 · Blog routine at every run and by any ad-hoc blog writing session. Field origin: the source brand's founder rejected a routine-published post as "a sales article introducing a product" — its outline WAS the product list (7 items, 7 cards, a section per product). This standard replaced it. Reader trust and AI-citation worthiness come from service journalism (the Wirecutter model): a post that dies when you remove the products was never an article.*

## The standard

**The article is never about the products.** It answers the reader's searched question with genuinely useful, experienced advice; your products appear only as natural examples or worked solutions inside that advice. The reader should finish smarter about the topic whether or not they click anything.

**Calibration example:**
- ❌ Salesy: "Our stunning arched floor lamp is the perfect statement piece for any room — shop now!"
- ✅ Subtle: "An arched floor lamp earns that empty corner — it adds height without eating floor space the way a second side table would (the exact silhouette I mean is in our lighting collection)."

## Hard caps (word-count enforceable)

- **Value-to-promotion ratio ≥ 90/10 by word count.** Product-referencing text (mention sentences + card text + collection pushes) stays ≤10% of the article.
- **Density cap: 1 linked product mention per ~300 words — max 4 per article** regardless of length. A "mention" = one sentence where the product is the example; it never gets its own section.
- **The outline is built from the topic's logic** (methods, rules, zones, mistakes, trade-offs) — never from a product list. No product-per-H2/H3 skeletons. A bottom-funnel post is a buying-ADVICE article (how to choose, what to avoid, honest trade-offs), never a catalog walk.
- **Product cards: max 2 per article** (hero + one), placed AFTER the advice paragraph they illustrate, never replacing it. Card label frames the card as the example ("The silhouette we mean: …"); button copy soft ("See it", never "Shop now").
- **One soft CTA maximum per article** — the closing `{{TAGLINE_CTA}}` block. Collection links elsewhere appear only as contextual anchors inside prose, never as CTAs.
- **Natural anchor text naming the product type** ("wavy ceramic vase in butter yellow"), never bare product names, never "click here".
- **Never open or close the article with a product.** The intro answers the reader's question with advice; the close hands them a next step in thinking.
- **Show, don't sell:** describe what the piece does to the room/user and the moment, with a reason or evidence attached (a spec number, a verified review line, your own measured data). Honest cons and "skip this" advice stay in — they are the trust asset.

## MANDATORY PRE-DELIVERY GATE

Score every draft against every line before delivering or publishing. Any FAIL → fix and re-run the gate. Never deliver a failing article.

**Structure**
1. The first ~100 words directly answer the searched question **with advice** — never with "here are our N products".
2. H2s map to the reader's real sub-questions (fan-out); each section opens with its answer. The outline is the topic's logic, never a product list.

**Ratio & placement**
3. Value-to-promotion ≥ 90/10 by word count.
4. ≤4 linked product mentions, ≤2 cards, exactly 1 soft CTA. No product opens or closes the article.

**Voice (experienced friend, not salesperson)**
5. Every claim carries a reason, an observation, or a cited source. Specific beats generic everywhere.
6. At least one honest trade-off, con, or "skip this" — sourced from real reviews or real experience.
7. First-party experience signals present (your measured data, review mining, real tests) — the things generic AI content can't fake.

**Banned patterns (automatic FAIL if present)**
- Pitch intros / roundup-promise intros as the article's thesis
- Feature dumps; spec lists in prose that don't answer a reader question
- "Look no further", "game-changer", "must-have", superlative spam ("stunning", "perfect", "gorgeous")
- Every-section-ends-in-a-product; product-per-H2 skeletons; stacked "Shop it" buttons
- Hard CTAs, urgency, scarcity ("Shop now!", "Don't miss out!")
- AI-slop tells: throat-clearing openers, "not X, it's Y" contrast chains, em-dash chains, adverb padding, vague declaratives

**The final gate — the delete-the-products self-test**
8. Mentally delete every product mention, card, and collection link. The article must still be complete, worth reading, and worth citing on its own. If anything collapses, the draft fails: restructure around the advice, then re-run this gate.

*(SEO/GEO rules — answer capsule, exact-phrase discipline, cited statistics, information gain, FAQ + JSON-LD — still apply per `playbooks/multi-engine-seo.md` and the 02 template's Phase B; this gate adds the editorial layer on top, it does not replace them.)*
